About Our Programs

We are Team 1247 – Blood, Sweat and Gears, a student-led STEM organization that competes in both the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) and the Aerial Drone Competition (ADC). Our mission is to innovate, lead, and inspire through hands-on engineering, coding, aeronautics, and community outreach.

Students learn real-world technical and team skills while designing industrial robots and autonomous drones to compete at regional, state, and national events.

Aerial Drone Competition Team

Our drone team designs, builds, and programs autonomous and manually controlled drones to compete in missions focused on precision flight, object retrieval, and autonomous navigation using computer vision.

2024 — Autonomous Mission Drone

  • Obstacle avoidance using Python/OpenCV
  • Waypoint tracking & autonomous flight control
  • Payload delivery mechanism

Flight System Specs

  • Controller: Raspberry Pi or Pixhawk (PX4)
  • Programming: Python/ArduPilot/MAVLink
  • Sensors: GPS, LiDAR, Barometer, IMU

We compete in the REC Foundation Aerial Drone Competition and other student engineering showcases.
